# GBRS Performance — What to Steal for Spencer

*Research synthesis, June 2026. Sources: GBRS Group human-performance pages, Vernon Griffith's TrainHeroic catalog (GBRS Performance, Loaded Mobility, Runners Mobility & Strength), the GBRS Performance Standards test breakdown, GBRS Group YouTube workout-breakdown videos (Pull Day, Leg Day, Press Day, Full-Body w/ DJ Shipley), and DJ Shipley's Huberman Lab episode "How to Make Yourself Unbreakable."*

---

## What GBRS Actually Does

GBRS Performance is a tactical-athlete program built by S&C coach **Vernon Griffith II** with DEVGRU vets DJ Shipley and Cole Fackler. Stripping the marketing, here's the real methodology:

- **Train for durability and longevity, not peaking.** The stated goal is to "stay in the game for a long career" — capability that holds up year-round, not a one-time test score. Shipley's framing on Huberman: you don't have to be Goggins; a 20-minute walk every day compounds. Consistency over intensity is the spine of the whole thing.

- **A real 5-day strength split, with conditioning bolted to the END.** Confirmed structure: **Mon** pull (trap-bar deadlift, grip, core) · **Tue** press (bench/incline) · **Wed** upper-lower dissociation + plyometrics · **Thu** heavy legs (belt squat, lunges, Bulgarian split squats) · **Fri** arms/shoulders "feel-good" accessory. **Sprints 2–3x/week placed at the end of sessions**, never as the main event. Sessions run lettered blocks (A–G) of mixed rep schemes.

- **Spine-friendly lower-body bias — by design, not accident.** They lead with the **trap bar deadlift** (not floor barbell DL) and **belt squat** (not barbell back squat), and lean heavily on **unilateral** work (Bulgarian split squats, lunges). This is convenient: GBRS's own staples already dodge the two movements Spencer's disc can't tolerate.

- **Carries and grip are a load-bearing pillar, not an afterthought.** Farmer's carry is a *test event* (bodyweight per hand). Programming rotates suitcase carry, front-rack carry, and goblet holds explicitly "to build a durable spine and challenge grip and posture." Loaded carries are how they train trunk stability and grip simultaneously.

- **Mobility is loaded and targeted (hip / ankle / T-spine / shoulder).** Griffith sells a standalone 4-day, 6-week mobility block running **15–20 min/day** to restore and then *strengthen* end-range — not passive stretching. This is almost certainly the front-loaded block Spencer bailed on: stacking that whole 15–20 min mobility session ON TOP of the lift inflates warm-ups to ~30 min. The fix is to mine it for movements, not run it as a separate session.

- **Pain literacy + aerobic base as injury insurance.** Shipley's signature point: learn the difference between *hurt* and *injured* so you train around niggles instead of stopping for six months. Aerobic base is built mostly through **Zone 2 walking** (low HR, high fat-ox, near-zero injury risk) — directly relevant to a fat-loss client with a bad back.

## What's Worth Stealing for Spencer

Ranked. Everything here respects his hard rules (disc-safe, hinge from pins/blocks only, no back squat, 40–60 min, no 30-min mobility tax) and leans on his sled / rings / heavy bag / carries.

1. **Loaded carry as the trunk/grip pillar (suitcase + front-rack rotation).** *What:* heavy unilateral carries replace dedicated core/grip work. *Slot:* finisher on Upper days — 4–5 sets of ~40-50ft heavy suitcase carry per side, rotate to front-rack monthly. *Why:* nails his weak grip AND trains anti-lateral-flexion trunk stability that protects the disc — no spinal loading, no floor work. GBRS's #1 transferable idea.

2. **Sprints/conditioning as an end-of-session finisher, never the main event.** *What:* 6–10 min of hard intervals tacked onto a lift. *Slot:* his existing conditioning day, plus optionally tack a short finisher onto one lift day. Use his **sled** (heavy pushes/drags, 6–8 x 20yd) or **airbike**, not running, to spare the back/ankle. *Why:* matches GBRS's "conditioning at completion" model and his fat-loss goal without adding a separate session.

3. **Trap-bar deadlift from blocks as his primary hinge.** *What:* GBRS's foundational pull, elevated. *Slot:* the A-movement on a Lower/pull day, pulled from pins/blocks (mid-shin+), 3–5 reps. *Why:* it's literally GBRS's signature posterior-chain lift, it's the most spine-neutral barbell hinge available, and it loads his "strong legs" + grip. Fits his pins/blocks rule cleanly.

4. **Belt squat + Bulgarian split squat as the leg engine.** *What:* GBRS's actual squat pattern — they don't back squat their athletes either. *Slot:* swap/confirm these as his Lower-day primaries (belt squat for axial-free bilateral load, BSS for unilateral). *Why:* zero spinal compression, builds the dense athletic legs he wants, directly mirrors GBRS Thursday leg day.

5. **A simple capability benchmark (steal the GBRS Standards, trimmed).** *What:* a short retest of pull-ups, bodyweight-rep bench, farmer carry distance, broad jump, 800m-equivalent (or sled). *Slot:* run it every 6–8 weeks, ~20 min, on a conditioning day. *Why:* gives "bulletproof/capable" an actual scoreboard, builds the pull-ups and grip he wants to grow, and it's the cleanest expression of GBRS's "test → train → retest" ethos.

6. **Mobility harvested INTO the lift, not bolted before it.** *What:* pull the 3–4 highest-value drills from GBRS's hip/ankle/T-spine block (loaded ankle dorsiflexion, 90/90 hip, T-spine rotation) and use them as **loaded warm-up sets / intra-set fillers**, not a standalone 30-min block. *Slot:* fold into his existing 6-min primer + rest periods. *Why:* keeps the GBRS mobility value but kills the exact time tax that made him quit. His ankle/hip limitations are the precise targets of these drills.

7. **Anti-rotation / heavy-bag work for trunk + power.** *What:* use his heavy bag for rotational power and conditioning (controlled strikes, bag carries/drags). *Slot:* alternate with sled on his conditioning finisher, or as an "active" recovery-day option. *Why:* trains rotary power and adds enjoyable conditioning he already likes, without spinal loading.

8. **Zone 2 walking as the recovery-day default.** *What:* DJ Shipley's literal #1 recommendation — 20–30 min brisk walk as real training. *Slot:* it's already his recovery/walk days — just make it deliberate Zone 2 (nasal-breathing pace). *Why:* maximizes fat-loss, builds aerobic base, zero injury/back risk. Already in his plan; GBRS validates keeping it.

9. **Pain-vs-injury rule + train-around mindset.** *What:* not a drill — an operating principle. Modify the movement, don't cancel the session, when a niggle (back/ankle) shows up. *Slot:* a standing rule in the app. *Why:* it's Shipley's core longevity message and the single most useful mindset for someone with a chronic disc issue.

## What to Skip

- **The standalone 30-min mobility session before the lift.** This is exactly what he bailed on. The value is in the *movements*, not the front-loaded block — harvest 3–4 drills (idea #6) and run them inside the lift instead.
- **Floor barbell deadlifts and any back squat.** GBRS doesn't even use these (they trap-bar and belt-squat), so there's nothing to steal here — and both violate his disc rules. Keep all hinging from pins/blocks.
- **Long rucks / loaded run volume.** GBRS's tactical roots include rucking and 800m run testing; both compress/jar the lumbar spine and tax his limited ankle. Substitute sled pushes and the airbike for the same conditioning stimulus.
- **The full 5-day strength template as-is.** He's on a 4-day Upper/Lower + 1 conditioning + 2 recovery structure that he likes and that fits 40–60 min. Don't re-architect to GBRS's 5-day split — port the *ideas* into his existing frame.

## Open Questions for Spencer

1. **Conditioning finisher — add one to a lift day, or keep it isolated?** GBRS bolts a 6–10 min sled/airbike finisher onto lift days (2–3x/wk). Want that on one upper day, or keep all conditioning on the dedicated day to protect your 40–60 min cap?
2. **Carry rotation — suitcase only, or rotate in front-rack/goblet monthly?** Suitcase is the highest-grip/anti-lateral-flexion bang. Want to keep it simple, or rotate variations the way GBRS does?
3. **Capability benchmark — in or out?** A 20-min GBRS-style retest every 6–8 weeks gives "bulletproof" a scoreboard (pull-ups, bench reps, carry distance, sled). Worth the one session, or skip the testing and just train?
