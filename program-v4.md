# Bulletproof — Program v4

*Spencer, 27, 5'11" ~195lb ~25%BF. Goal: fat loss + lean/dense/athletic. Herniated lumbar disc, APT, weak ankle/hip mobility, hip weakness, weak grip. Strong legs, decent upper body. ~6am sessions, 40-60 min, full commercial gym + sled/rings/heavy bag.*

**Hard rules (never violated anywhere in this spec):** all hinging from rack pins/blocks at mid-shin — NEVER the floor · NO barbell back squat · no loaded spinal flexion · no end-range loaded extension · brace, don't bend (move at the hips, lumbar stays neutral).

---

## Program Logic

- **Biweekly A/B rotation, one program that breathes.** Block A (Strength, Weeks 1-2): heavier compounds, lower reps, fewer accessories, longer rests — quality over volume. Block B (Hypertrophy/Variation, Weeks 3-4): different variations and angles so it feels fresh, higher reps, more single-leg, more core, shorter rests, pump finishers. After B you rotate back to A heavier than the previous A. This keeps the joints/disc fresh, drives both strength and size, and stops staleness — the thing that made the old plan feel "thrown together" was no rotation and no logic; this is the logic.
- **A vs B is real, not just numbers.** The primary lifts, accessory emphasis, and rep ranges actually change between blocks (e.g. V-squat heavy 5s in A → Smith heel-elevated + leg press higher-rep in B; trap-bar heavy from blocks in A → B-stance/single-leg + RDL volume in B). Same movement patterns, different expression.
- **Progress within a block, then jump between blocks.** Week 1 establishes working loads. Week 2 adds one rep per set OR a small load bump at the same reps (double-progression). When A comes back around, start heavier than the last A. Leave 1-2 reps in the tank on every working set — never grind form into flexion.
- **Two separate back systems, don't confuse them.** (1) **Daily 5 = spine hygiene** — the McGill Big 3 + glute bridge + hip-flexor stretch, done most days as endurance/control work, NOT trained to failure (reference section). (2) **Trained core block = a programmed block inside sessions** — anti-extension / anti-rotation isometrics in Block A, more dynamic + added hanging/ab-wheel volume in Block B. Spine hygiene keeps the back calm daily; the core block builds the posture-protective trunk strength he's missing.
- **Deload every 8 weeks** (after 2 full A/B cycles): one easier week — keep the lifts, cut sets ~40%, drop load ~10%, keep movement quality high. Conditioning stays Zone 2 only that week.

## The Week

> Toggle Block A (Weeks 1-2) or Block B (Weeks 3-4) at the top. Each training day fits 40-60 min. The Primer (~6 min, includes Daily 5 pieces) runs before every lift.

### Day 1 — Lower A: Squat / Quad

#### Block A — Strength
- Pendulum / V-squat — 5×5 (top set 4-6) — sit between heels and balls of feet, control the eccentric, full depth without pelvic tuck.
- Leg press (feet low/narrow, quad bias) — 3×6-8 — stop depth before the low back lifts off the pad.
- Bulgarian split squat (DBs) — 3×6-8/side — torso 25-35° forward, push through the whole foot, heel down.
- Leg extension — 3×8-10 — control the eccentric, brief pause at the top.
- Seated calf raise — 3×10-12 — full stretch and squeeze, pause both ends (seated = spine unloaded).
- **Finisher:** Sled push — 4×20yd, moderate-heavy — long spine, drive through the legs.
- **Core block (A):** Hollow-body hold 3×20-30s · Pallof press 3×8/side (slow, long holds).

#### Block B — Hypertrophy
- Smith heel-elevated squat — 4×10-12 — heels on plates, tall torso, constant tension, no pause at lockout.
- Heel-elevated goblet squat — 3×12-15 — ribs stacked, elbows up, controlled.
- Walking / reverse lunge (DBs) — 3×12/side — reverse lunge if knees cranky, smooth turnaround.
- Leg extension (1.5 reps or slow eccentric) — 3×12-15 — chase the pump, back pinned to pad.
- Standing single-leg calf raise — 3×15/side — full ROM off a step.
- **Finisher:** Backward sled drag — 3×20yd — upright, knee-friendly quad burn.
- **Core block (B):** Hanging knee/leg raise (PPT) 3×10-12 · Side plank 3×30s/side · Ab wheel rollout (kneeling, partial) 2×8.

### Day 2 — Upper A: Push

#### Block A — Strength
- Barbell bench press — 5×5 (top set 4-6) — elbows tucked 45-60°, shoulder blades pinned, small natural arch only.
- Standing/seated DB shoulder press (seated, supported by default) — 4×6-8 — ribs down, brace; no lumbar arch to finish.
- Ring dips — 3×6-8 (add load/assist as needed) — elbows track back, shoulders packed, don't sink into pain.
- Cable lateral raise — 3×10-12 — lead with the elbow, drive width out not up.
- Triceps pushdown — 3×8-10 — elbows pinned, only the forearm moves.
- **Finisher (conditioning, this is the one sled/airbike finisher on an upper day):** Airbike or sled intervals — 6×20s hard / 60s easy — crisp, short, leave the back fresh.
- **Core block (A):** Reverse plank 3×20-30s (neutral, glute-driven) · Suitcase carry 3×30m/side (anti-lateral-flexion + grip).

#### Block B — Hypertrophy
- Incline DB press (~30°) — 4×10-12 — slight elbow tuck at the bottom, big stretch.
- Machine shoulder press — 3×12-15 — fixed path, push near failure safely.
- Cable lateral raise (lean-away, single arm) — 4×12-20 — peak stretch, higher volume (his #1 width driver).
- Ring / parallel-bar dips — 3×AMRAP-2 — controlled depth.
- Overhead cable triceps extension — 3×12-15 — long-head stretch, full range.
- **Finisher (conditioning, sled/airbike on the upper day):** Airbike — 8-10 min steady-hard intervals (e.g. 30s/30s) — fat-loss engine without frying the legs.
- **Core block (B):** Pallof press 3×12/side (dynamic) · Suitcase carry 3×40m/side (lighter, longer).

### Day 3 — Conditioning

*Not block-dependent — runs the same in A and B. ~30-45 min, spine-and-ankle-friendly. Keep it short and crisp so it doesn't tax the lift days.*

- **Sled intervals** — 6-8×20yd push/drag, full-ish rest — long spine, moderate load.
- **Heavy bag** — 5-8×60-90s rounds — controlled rotation, core braced; favor pace/volume over max-power hooks.
- **Zone 2 finish** — 15-20 min incline treadmill walk or bike — conversational, ~60-70% max HR.
- **Grip add-on** — Plate pinch carries 3×20-30m (pinch grip) — hold cleanly, stand tall, no shrug.
- *Alternate sled ↔ bag week to week so it stays fresh. Every 6-8 weeks, optionally swap this day for the capability benchmark (pull-ups, bench reps, farmer carry distance, broad jump, sled time).*

### Day 4 — Lower B: Hinge / Posterior

#### Block A — Strength
- Trap-bar deadlift from blocks/pins (mid-shin) — 5×5 (top set 4-6) — brace, hips back, neutral spine, drive the floor away; NEVER from the floor.
- Hip thrust — 4×6-8 — finish in posterior pelvic tilt, ribs down, no lumbar hyperextension at lockout.
- Lying hamstring curl — 3×8-10 — hips pinned, control the eccentric.
- 45° back extension (bodyweight, neutral finish) — 3×10-12 — rise only to a straight line, never arch past neutral.
- Standing calf raise (or leg-press calf) — 3×10-12 — full ROM; prefer leg-press calf to keep axial load off the spine.
- **Finisher:** Heavy farmer's carry (trap-bar or DBs) — 3×30m heavy — stand tall, brace, support grip.
- **Core block (A):** Side plank 3×30-40s/side · Dead bug 3×8/side (anti-extension, low back pressed down).

#### Block B — Hypertrophy
- RDL from pins (mid-shin) — 4×10-12 — hips back, soft knees, bar drags the legs, stop before the back position changes. (Swap: trap-bar RDL from elevated handles.)
- B-stance / single-leg hip thrust — 3×12/side — controlled, glute-driven, no rib flare.
- Seated hamstring curl — 3×12-15 — hip-flexed stretch, control down.
- Cable glute kickback — 3×15/side — move only at the hip, torso still, no low-back arch to throw the leg.
- Single-leg calf raise — 3×15/side — full stretch, pause top.
- **Finisher:** Cable pull-through — 2×15-20 — hip-hinge pump, low spinal load.
- **Core block (B):** Hanging leg raise (PPT) 3×10-12 · Reverse plank 3×30s · Ab wheel rollout (kneeling) 3×10.

### Day 5 — Upper A: Pull

#### Block A — Strength
- Pull-up (assisted/banded to build from 2-3 reps) — 5×3-5 — full dead-hang to chin-over, pack the lats first, no kipping.
- Chest-supported row — 4×6-8 — chest stays on the pad, drive elbows back, squeeze.
- Neutral-grip lat pulldown — 3×8-10 — stay fairly upright, full stretch at top under control.
- Face pull / rear-delt fly — 3×12-15 — light, elbows high, pull toward eyes, "spread the rope."
- Fat-grip hammer curl — 3×8-10 — crush grip, strict, lighten if grip ends the set early.
- **Finisher:** Fat-grip dead hang — 3×max time — slight scapular pack, build time progressively (support grip).
- **Core block (A):** *(optional 3rd core day)* Pallof press 3×8/side · Hollow-body hold 2×20-30s.

#### Block B — Hypertrophy
- Neutral-grip lat pulldown — 4×10-12 — pull to upper chest, control the stretch (more volume to build toward bodyweight pull-ups).
- Ring row — 3×12-15 — straight plank line, chest to rings, full squeeze.
- Single-arm cable pulldown — 3×12-15/side — big stretch, fix imbalances.
- Reverse pec-deck (rear delt) — 3×15-20 — light, shoulders down, posture work.
- Incline DB curl — 3×12-15 — bigger stretch; superset with rope hammer curl 3×15 for the pump.
- **Finisher:** Towel-over-bar hang — 3×max time — grip/decompression.
- **Core block (B):** *(optional)* Ab wheel rollout 2×10 · Side plank 2×40s/side.

### Day 6 — Recovery: Mobility + Zone 2 Walk

*No lifting. ~30-40 min total.*
- **Full Daily 5** — modified curl-up, side plank, bird dog, glute bridge ISO, couch/half-kneeling hip-flexor stretch (5-10 min; descending pyramid, endurance not failure).
- **Mobility** — knee-to-wall ankle dorsiflexion 2×10/side · 90/90 hip switches 2×5/side · couch stretch 2×30-45s/side (posterior tilt) · cat-cow + open book (gentle, pain-free).
- **Zone 2 walk** — 20-30 min brisk/incline, nasal-breathing pace. Real training for fat loss, zero back/ankle risk.

### Day 7 — Recovery: Mobility + Zone 2 Walk

*Same template as Day 6 — keep it deliberate, not skipped. The walk is the point; the mobility is the insurance.*
- **Daily 5** (full) + **passive bar hang** 2-3×max time (decompression, light scapular pack).
- **Mobility** — repeat Day 6 set, bias whatever felt tightest in the week (ankle or hip).
- **Zone 2 walk** — 20-30 min. Optional: easy bag/shadowbox round if he wants movement, kept light.

## Core Block

The trained-core block lives **inside the two lower days (Day 1, Day 4)** and optionally one upper day (Day 5). It is distinct from the **Daily 5** spine-hygiene flow (reference section). All movements are disc-safe: anti-extension, anti-rotation, anti-lateral-flexion — never loaded spinal flexion. **NO sit-ups, crunches, or Russian twists** (loaded flexion = the disc-injury mechanism).

**Block A usage:** isometric, lower reps, longer holds — anti-extension (hollow hold, reverse plank, dead bug) and anti-rotation (Pallof) to build bracing endurance and posture under tension.
**Block B usage:** more reps and more dynamic — add hanging leg raise and ab-wheel volume, longer/lighter carries, dynamic Pallof. More total core volume to drive the trunk development he wants.

| Movement | Cue | Subs (busy-station / minimal-equip / disc-safer) | Watch for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pallof press** (anti-rotation) | Press the handle straight out and resist the cable twisting you — brace, don't rotate. | Band Pallof (minimal-equip) · Banded anti-rotation hold static (busy) · Half-kneeling Pallof (disc-safer, locks the pelvis) | Letting the torso rotate toward the stack — that defeats the point; go lighter and stay square. Holding breath — exhale slow through the hold. |
| **Side plank** (anti-lateral-flexion) | Straight line head-to-feet, hips lifted, brace — don't sag or rotate. | Knees-bent side plank (disc-safer regression) · Suitcase carry (minimal-equip, trains the same anti-side-bend) · Cable side-bend resist (busy) | Hips sagging or twisting — lift the hip, stack the shoulders. Cranking the neck — keep it long. |
| **Dead bug** (anti-extension) | Low back pressed gently into the floor, brace, then move the opposite arm/leg slow. | Dead bug feet-supported (regression) · Bird dog (minimal-equip, same anti-extension/rotation) · Pallof (busy, anti-rotation alt) | Low back arching off the floor as the limb lowers — shorten the reach, re-brace. Speeding up — slow and controlled is the skill. |
| **Hollow-body hold** (anti-extension) | Low back glued to the floor, ribs down, legs/arms extended only as far as you can keep it flat. | Tuck-hold (knees in, regression) · Dead bug (disc-safer alt) · Bench leg-lower hold (busy) | Low back peeling off the floor — bend the knees / raise the arms until it stays flat. Holding breath. |
| **Hanging leg raise (PPT)** (anti-extension, dynamic) | Tuck the pelvis under (PPT) FIRST, then raise — initiate from the abs, never let the low back arch at the bottom. | Hanging knee raise (regression) · Captain's chair / lying leg raise neutral spine (disc-safer, back supported) · Lying leg raise (minimal-equip) | A low-back arch at the bottom = abs can't hold the tuck; regress to knee raises. Swinging/momentum — control both directions. |
| **Reverse plank** (anti-extension iso, posterior) | Hips up to a straight line, glutes ON, ribs down — a moderate neutral isometric, NOT an end-range arch. | Tabletop reverse plank (knees bent, regression) · Glute bridge ISO (disc-safer, same posterior iso) · Bench-supported reverse plank (busy) | Sagging into a deep lumbar arch — keep it neutral, glute-driven; **flag and skip if there's any acute low-back pain.** Shoulders shrugging. |
| **Ab wheel rollout** (advanced anti-extension) | Kneeling, partial range first — ribs down, brace hard, never let the low back arch as you roll out. | Kneeling partial-range / towel-under-wheel (regression) · Dead bug (disc-safer substitute) · Anti-extension plank (busy) | Low back arching/hips dropping at full reach — cut the range, that's the disc-danger position. Only progress range when neutral holds. |

## New Movements

*New (not in research-content.md) movements, in the source's format: one-line cue, SUBS (tagged), WATCH-FOR (spine-protection first).*

### Pendulum / V-squat (Day 1 primary)
**Cue:** Stay mid-foot, control the eccentric, hit full depth WITHOUT the pelvis tucking under — the machine's arc keeps the torso supported.
**Subs:**
- Smith heel-elevated squat — busy-station; fixed path, heels up to spare ankle/back.
- Heel-elevated goblet squat — minimal-equip; one DB, same quad bias, tall torso.
- Leg press — disc-safer regression; back fully supported, zero axial load.

**Watch for:**
- Letting the low back round / pelvis tuck at the bottom (butt wink) — stop depth before it happens; loaded lumbar flexion is the disc risk.
- Knees caving in — track them over the 2nd/3rd toe.

### Reverse plank (core block)
**Cue:** Hips up to a straight shoulder-to-heel line, glutes ON, ribs down — a moderate neutral isometric, never an end-range arch.
**Subs:**
- Tabletop reverse plank (knees bent) — disc-safer regression; shorter lever, easier to keep neutral.
- Glute bridge ISO — minimal-equip; same posterior isometric with the spine fully supported.
- Bench-supported reverse plank (hands on bench) — busy/minimal; reduces the demand.

**Watch for:**
- Sagging into a deep lumbar arch — keep it neutral and glute-driven. **Flag and skip entirely if there's any acute low-back pain** — for a disc this is the one core move to drop first if symptoms flare.
- Shoulders shrugging up to the ears — pack the shoulders down.

### Hollow-body hold (core block)
**Cue:** Low back glued to the floor, ribs down, extend the limbs only as far as you can keep it flat.
**Subs:**
- Tuck hold (knees pulled in) — disc-safer regression; shortest lever.
- Dead bug — minimal-equip; the dynamic version of the same anti-extension skill.
- Bench leg-lower hold — busy; supported variation.

**Watch for:**
- Low back peeling off the floor — the spine-protection failure point; bend the knees or raise the arms until it stays flat.
- Holding the breath — breathe shallow and steady through the hold.

### Ab wheel rollout (core block, advanced)
**Cue:** Kneeling, partial range to start — brace hard, ribs down, never let the low back arch as you roll out.
**Subs:**
- Kneeling partial-range / towel-under-wheel — disc-safer regression; caps the range so neutral holds.
- Dead bug — minimal-equip; same anti-extension demand without the wheel.
- Anti-extension plank (long-lever) — busy; static version.

**Watch for:**
- Low back arching and hips dropping at full reach — that IS the disc-danger position; cut the range immediately, only extend it once neutral is rock-solid.
- Rolling out faster than you can pull back — control both directions.

### Hanging leg raise (core block, dynamic)
**Cue:** Tuck the pelvis under (posterior pelvic tilt) FIRST, then raise — initiate from the abs, never let the low back arch at the bottom.
**Subs:**
- Hanging knee raise — disc-safer regression; less leverage, easier to control the tuck.
- Captain's chair / lying leg raise with neutral spine — disc-safer; back supported, removes spinal compression.
- Lying leg raise (floor) — minimal-equip; hands under hips, keep the low back down.

**Watch for:**
- A low-back arch at the bottom of the rep — means the abs can't hold the PPT; regress to knee raises (if you can't avoid the arch, the move is too advanced).
- Swinging/using momentum — control the lift AND the lower; straight-leg compresses the spine more, so progress gradually.
